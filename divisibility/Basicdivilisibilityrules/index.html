Basic Divisibility Rules

Here are the most basic divisibility rules. Do note that if you have a divisibility rule for a number like 12, the number must work for both the divisibility rule of 3 and 4

Divisibility by 2

The number must end in 0 , 2 , 4 , 6 or 8

For any number abc......x = abc......0 + x. Hence abc......0 must be divisible by 2 and thus to determine whether or not a number is divisible by 2 depends completely on the last digit x.

Divisibility by 3

The sum of digits must be divisible by 3

Notice how 10 = 9 + 1. Therefore 10^n would also have a remainder of 1 when divided by 9. So for any number, the sum of digits determines if the number is divisible by 3. This can be shown very easily for as many digits as you want. But for simplicity's sake, we will do it with 3 since abc = 100a + 10b + c = 99a + 9b + c =9(11a + b) + a + b + c

Divisibility by 4

The last two digits must be divisible by 4

Similarly to the proof for divisibility by 2, we realise that any number abc......xy = abc......00 + xy. Since abc......00 is divisible by 4, seeing if a number abc......xy is divisible by 4 depends completely on xy

Divisibility by 5

The last digit must be 0 or 5

Similarly to the proof for divisibility by 2, notice that for any number abc......x = abc......0 + x. Hence abc......0 must be divisible by 5 and thus to determine whether or not a number is divisible by 5 depends completely on the last digit x. Since 5 and 0 are the only digits divisible by 5, if those numbers are x, it would be divisible by 5.

Divisibility by 8

The last 3 digits must be divisible by 8

Similarly to the proof for divisibility by 2, we realise that any number abc......xyz = abc......000 + xyz. Since abc......000 is divisible by 4, seeing if a number abc......xyz is divisible by 4 depends completely on xyz

Divisibility by 9

The sum of digits must be divisible by 9

Notice how 10 = 9 + 1. Therefore 10^n would also have a remainder of 1 when divided by 9. So for any number, the sum of digits determines if the number is divisible by 9. This can be shown very easily for as many digits as you want. But for simplicity's sake, we will do it with 3 since abc = 100a + 10b + c = 99a + 9b + c =9(11a + b) + a + b + c

Divisibility by 11

The difference between the sum of odd and even digits is divisible by 11

Notice how 10 = 11 - 1. This results in the proof. Don't worry, more details will be covered in the Modular Arithmetic Portion.

Divisibility by 13

They are in the form of abcabcabc...abc where there are an even number of "abc"s

Notice that 1001 = 7 x 11 x 13. Since abcabc = 1001 x abc and any abcabc...... where there are an even number of "abc"s is a multiple of abcabc. Hence, any abcabc...... with an even number of "abc"s is divisible by 13.
