Q1. Prerequisites: Basic Combinatoric Calculations : The Choose Function

Here is one of my favourite questions involving the choose function

Find the number of rectangles in this figure. Try it before looking at the solution!

For this question, we note that any 2 points on each side of the whole figure, when lines are drawn through the selected points, rectangles would be formed. There are 5 points on the length of the figure and 4 points on the breadth of the figure. Therefore, we choose 2 points among the 5 points for the length and 2 points among the 4 points for the breadth. The answer would be 5C2x4C2=60.
Note: this question could also be solved by counting manually. However this takes a long time so it is recommended to use the method above.

Q2. Prerequisites: Basic Combinatoric Calculations : The Choose Function

Here's another question:

#

An ant is travelling on the following grid. It can only move right or upwards. Given the ant starts at point A and needs to reach point B, how many ways are there for the ant to reach point B?

If we let a right movement be R and a upward movement be U, one possible way for the ant to move from A to B is RRRRRUUUU. Now notice for each of the routes, there must be 5 'R's and 4 'U's.
So, we only have to choose when we move in the upward direction and the rest will be right movements. Since we have 9 total movements, the possible number of movements 9C4 = 126
There are other methods, but this is by far the simplest.
